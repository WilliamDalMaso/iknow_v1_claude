0007 — Douglass Gate-Closing Plan (Step 1, diagnosis only)

When: 2026-05-29 (commit c7a2e54)

Why

To honestly claim the system "safely and cleanly extracts and prepares a book for mapping/embedding/graph," Douglass must flip from safe_for_downstream: false to true. This step reads the existing diagnostics and turns them into a concrete plan — review-only, no behavior change.

What I found

  • Of the 39 bbox/span cases, three queues are already resolved (front-matter 9, visual 7, gold-gaps 0). Only one group of 23 blocks: likely-true paragraph grouping defects — two real paragraphs accidentally merged into one (e.g. cp_000025 p21, cp_000032 p29 which runs through "…in the following words:—" into a song).
  • The correction-design scaffold (narrow_grouping_correction_design.json) is empty (confirmed_defects: 0) — the visual confirmation + design was never completed. That is the gap.
  • Fix class (per the remediation plan): a narrow, guarded paragraph-split, not a broad merge-policy rewrite. The needed signals (line gap, indentation, terminal punctuation) are already computed.

The plan to flip the flag

  1. Confirm the 23 visually (review-only).
  2. Author gold paragraph-boundary rows for the affected pages (review-only).
  3. Design + implement the guarded split — changes extraction behavior; needs explicit OK.
  4. Prove at the gates: improves vs gold, no audit-safety regression, side-effect review.
  5. Re-run, re-verify against the regression fingerprint, flip safe_for_downstream.
  6. Then downstream (mapping → embedding → graph) legitimately opens.

Result

Full plan at gate_closing_plan.html. Steps 1–2 are in-lane (no behavior change); Step 3 onward needs authorization + the gold gates.
