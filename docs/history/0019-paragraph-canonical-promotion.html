Paragraph Canonical Promotion

Date: 2026-05-27

Purpose

Phase 1 can now inspect, review, and correct candidate streams. This step adds the first narrow candidate-to-canonical gate for Douglass paragraph candidates only.

Canonical does not mean perfect. It means evidence-bound, audited enough under current rules, and safe to use downstream. This gate promotes paragraphs only.

What Changed

  1. Added canonical_paragraphs.jsonl for promoted paragraph candidates.
  2. Added promotion_blockers.jsonl for candidates not promoted by the paragraph gate.
  3. Added canonical_promotion_report.json for counts, warning policy, and validation status.
  4. Updated the audit to show promotion summary, warning counts, blocker samples, and per-object promotion status.
  5. Updated validation so canonical paragraphs must trace to paragraph candidates and exclude structure, page artifacts, and unknown objects.

What Did Not Change

  1. No structure was promoted.
  2. No OCR was added.
  3. No AI/model review was added.
  4. No embeddings, retrieval, graph work, user-facing answers, or broad automation were added.

Remaining Weakness

The first gate is conservative but still deterministic. It proves the promotion mechanism, not perfect paragraph purity. Future work must review the promoted paragraph layer against visual evidence before downstream intelligence depends on it.
