Cross-Page Continuation Experiment

Date: 2026-05-27

Purpose

The authoritative gold set changed the diagnosis from broad paragraph merging to cross-page over-splitting. This step tests a narrow deterministic experiment named v2_cross_page_continuation.

This is experiment-only. The active canonical extraction policy remains v1_consecutive_lines. No OCR, AI review, embeddings, retrieval, graph work, user-facing answers, or structure promotion was added.

Policy Tested

The experiment joins the last body paragraph candidate on page N with the first body paragraph candidate on page N+1 when the previous paragraph looks incomplete, the next paragraph looks like continuation text, and no structure candidate intervenes after page furniture is ignored.

Gold Result

  1. Baseline gold matched paragraphs: 5.
  2. Experimental gold matched paragraphs: 11.
  3. Baseline gold over-split paragraphs: 6.
  4. Experimental gold over-split paragraphs: 0.
  5. Baseline gold over-merged paragraphs: 0.
  6. Experimental gold over-merged paragraphs: 0.
  7. Baseline paragraph precision/recall: 0.455.
  8. Experimental paragraph precision/recall: 1.000.
  9. Object-label accuracy remained 1.000.

Audit Safety Result

  1. Cross-page joins: 79.
  2. Rejected cross-page candidates: 26.
  3. BBox span risk improved from 74 to 40.
  4. Likely true accidental merges improved from 49 to 23.
  5. Canonical promoted paragraphs changed from 205 to 122.
  6. Downstream safety remains false.

Outcome

The report marks the experiment as adoptable under the current gold and audit-safety checks, but adoption is not automatic. The large number of joins and the drop in promoted canonical paragraph count need review before the policy becomes active.

Next Step

Review joined cross-page examples in phase1_audit.html, especially the cases outside the current gold pages. If the joins are clean, the next change can promote v2_cross_page_continuation from experiment to active policy with an explicit history entry.
