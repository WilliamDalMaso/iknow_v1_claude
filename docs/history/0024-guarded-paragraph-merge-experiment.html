Guarded Paragraph Merge Experiment

Date: 2026-05-27

Purpose

The bbox span decision summary showed many likely accidental paragraph merges. This step tested a narrow deterministic paragraph merge policy named v2_span_guarded against the existing v1_consecutive_lines baseline.

This was an experiment, not a broad extractor rewrite. OCR, AI review, embeddings, retrieval, graph work, user-facing answers, and structure promotion remain excluded.

Policy Tested

The experimental policy adds guarded split conditions around large vertical gaps, large visual spans after terminal lines, unusual line count, uppercase starts, and indentation evidence. The existing baseline remains the active policy for canonical outputs because the experiment did not improve risk.

Douglass Result

  1. Baseline paragraph candidates: 349.
  2. Experimental paragraph candidates: 395.
  3. Baseline promoted canonical paragraphs: 205.
  4. Experimental promoted canonical paragraphs: 270.
  5. Baseline bbox span risk count: 74.
  6. Experimental bbox span risk count: 91.
  7. Baseline likely true accidental merge count: 49.
  8. Experimental likely true accidental merge count: 61.
  9. Paragraphs split by the experimental policy: 42.
  10. Possible over-splitting risks: 26.
The experimental policy made the measured risk worse, so it was not adopted as the active canonical extraction behavior. The active manifest records v1_consecutive_lines as the current paragraph merge policy and v2_span_guarded only as the experiment policy.

What Worked

  1. The project can now compare paragraph merge policies without hiding the baseline.
  2. The report captures before/after counts, split examples, and possible over-splitting risk.
  3. The system refused to promote a worse experiment into canonical behavior.

What Failed

The simple span-guarded policy split more paragraphs, but the review layer still saw more bbox span risk and more likely true accidental merges. This suggests the correction target is not just line count or vertical span. The next correction should inspect split examples and likely merge pages visually before attempting another merge rule.

Next Step

Review a small set of pages where the experiment worsened risk and compare the baseline paragraph, experimental split children, page image, and bounding boxes. The next merge policy should be based on those observed failure modes, not only on generic span thresholds.
